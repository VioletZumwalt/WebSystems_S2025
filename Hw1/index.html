A Memorable Gaming Experience

Once, when I was younger, I ran a DND game that was centered around a homebrewed plotline. I had told my players off the bat that I was going to lie to them, that the focus was figuring out who was lying and when. That it wasn't combat.
They did not listen to this statement, and never once tried to figure out who was lying and when. They attacked people wildly.
In the end, they ended up being TPKed because one trusted a mindflayer willingly, and it very quickly wiped out the party.
That room was intended as a stealth section.
They never wanted to play dnd with me again.

Details About the Game

Game Title Dungeons and Dragons
Platform Tabletop
Players Present Rogue, Cleric, Warlock, Barbarian
Duration 15 hours total.

What Made It Special

How does one completely ignore the warnings a DM gives, and then gets mad when the warnings were correct? One that I do not know, but it's very funny never being spoken to again because they refused to listen!

Why are games important

Even when a player is being completely silly in a situation like this, games like these are important because they're distractions from current issues, such as politics!
Thank goodness we haven't had any major political situations happen within the last few days that causes a need for this distraction, haha!
It helps having something to fall back to as a comfort and escapism, and a way to bond with people, even if it goes poorly like that DND session
